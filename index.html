Why harmonics appear

A pulsar emits a periodic pulse with period P. If the pulse is narrow (small duty cycle δ), it cannot be represented by a single sinusoid at the fundamental frequency f₀ = 1/P. Instead, the Fourier series requires many harmonics at f₀, 2f₀, 3f₀, … to reconstruct the sharp pulse shape.

The number of significant harmonics is approximately Nharm ≈ 1/δ. A 5% duty cycle produces ~20 harmonics; a 1% duty cycle produces ~100. A perfectly sinusoidal signal (δ = 50%) has power only at the fundamental.

In pulsar searches, harmonic summing adds power from multiple harmonics to increase sensitivity to narrow-pulse signals that would be missed by searching only at the fundamental frequency.
